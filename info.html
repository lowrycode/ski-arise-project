Accommodation

There are different types of accommodation available at ski resorts. When browsing our packages, you will see the following types of accommodation on offer:

Hotels

Available at various price points, these are best for travelers looking for convenience and comfort.

Typical features include:

  • On-site restaurants
  • Room service
  • Leisure facilities

Chalets

Standalone, cabin-style accommodations, often with a rustic yet cozy feel. Families or groups looking for privacy and a home-like environment.

Typical features include:

  • Kitchens for self-catering
  • Living areas with fireplaces
  • Options range from basic to luxury

Lodges

A mix between a hotel and a cabin, often located near the slopes. A great choice for those looking for a blend of comfort and a rustic atmosphere.

Typical features include:

  • On-site dining options
  • Communal lounges
  • Sometimes include ski-in/ski-out access

Apartments

Self-contained units with kitchen facilities, often part of larger complexes. A great choice for families or groups who prefer cooking their own meals.

Typical features include:

  • Access to resort amenities (e.g., pools, hot tubs)
  • Private or shared spaces
  • Ski lockers for equipment storage

Cabins

Smaller, often more rustic accommodations set apart from larger developments. Great for couples or small groups seeking a quiet retreat.

Typical features include:

  • Basic to luxury options available
  • Fireplaces or wood stoves
  • Ideal for secluded, nature-focused stays

Hostels

Budget-friendly shared accommodations. A great choice for solo travelers, young skiers, or those on a tight budget.

Typical features include:

  • Dormitory-style rooms
  • Shared bathrooms and kitchens
  • Social environment, ideal for meeting fellow travelers

Beginners

Skiing can be an exciting and rewarding sport, but for beginners and those who have never skied before, it's important to understand the basics, prepare properly, and set realistic expectations. Here are some useful tips and information to get started:

Choosing Your Holiday

When choosing your holiday, look for a resort which has beginner-friendly slopes. Slopes are given a colour rating to indicate their level of difficulty. You should look out for the green slopes.

You can find more information about slope categories here.

Equipment

Beginners don't need to invest in expensive equipment. You can rent skis, poles and boots from the ski resort or a rental shop.

All of the packages that we recommend for beginners have this included in the price of the holiday. We will sort all of these details for you before you go on your trip.

Clothing

It is important to take the right clothing on a ski holiday.

Dress in layers: a moisture-wicking base layer, an insulating mid-layer, and a waterproof outer layer. Wear ski socks (not regular socks) to prevent blisters.

The following essentials will be provided for you when you pick up the rest of your ski equipment from the rental shop:

  • waterproof gloves
  • helmet
  • goggles
  • neck warmer or scarf

Ski Schools

We highly recommend getting lessons from a professional ski instructor. Even a single beginner lesson can significantly improve your experience.

All of the packages that we recommend for beginners have well respected ski schools at the resort. For other packages, you can see if a ski school is available by looking at the Resort Information provided with each package (on the All Packages page).

Since our customers have a range of abilities and requirements, we do not include ski lessons in the price of our packages (unless stated otherwise) so you will need to take this into account when budgeting for your holiday.

Safety Tips

Know the rules of the slopes:

  • Yield to skiers below you.
  • Stop only at the side of the trail where you're visible.
  • Stay on marked trails.

Protect Yourself:

  • Always wear a helmet to reduce the risk of head injuries.
  • Use sunscreen to protect your skin from UV rays, which are strong at high altitudes.
  • Stay hydrated and take regular breaks - skiing at altitude can be exhausting.
  • Familiarize yourself with how to get on and off ski lifts. Ask for help if you're unsure.

Enjoy Yourself!

Focus on enjoying the experience rather than mastering techniques right away.

Celebrate every small improvement, like completing a run without falling or successfully stopping.

By taking it slow, staying safe, and preparing well, your first ski trip can be a fun and memorable adventure!

Slopes

To get the best skiing experience, it is important to understand some basic information about ski slopes.

Slope Difficulty Ratings

Ski slopes are categorized by difficulty levels, often marked with color-coded signs that vary slightly by region. In Europe, the following colours are used:

  • Green: Very easy. Beginner slopes.
  • Blue: Easy. For those with basic skills.
  • Red: Intermediate. Steeper and more challenging.
  • Black: Advanced. Steep and for experienced skiers.

Weather and Snow Conditions

Weather can significantly affect the slope:

  • Fresh Snow: Ideal for skiing but might be harder to navigate for beginners.
  • Icy Conditions: Hard, slippery snow can be challenging and dangerous.
  • Visibility: Fog or heavy snow can make navigation difficult.
  • Wind: High winds might close lifts or expose icy patches.

Although we cannot guarantee good weather conditions, we only choose resorts that have a reputation for good quality snow.
